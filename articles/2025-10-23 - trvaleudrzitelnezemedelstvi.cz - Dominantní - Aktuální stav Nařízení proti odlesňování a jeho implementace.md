## Text článku

EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR): Current Status and Implementation

**Regulation Overview**

The EUDR regulation (EU 2023/1115) covers seven commodities linked to global deforestation, including beef and cattle products alongside cacoa, coffee, palm oil, soya, wood, and natural rubber.

**Beef and Animal Agriculture Connection**

Cattle and beef production represent one of the primary commodities targeted by the regulation. The regulation applies not only to raw meat but to all derivative products including leather goods and processed foods containing beef.

**Core Requirements**

Products entering the EU market must satisfy three conditions:
1. No deforestation after December 31, 2020
2. Legal production compliance
3. Due diligence documentation covering geolocation data

**Implementation Timeline**

- December 30, 2025: Regulation enters force
- June 30, 2026: Enforcement begins for large enterprises
- December 30, 2026: Enforcement begins for small and micro-enterprises

**Industry Perspective**

Czech agricultural associations argue the regulation creates "administrative burden" and may redirect high-risk products to non-EU markets rather than eliminating deforestation globally. However, the regulation represents a significant step toward ensuring that animal products and their derivatives do not contribute to deforestation.

The regulation requires detailed tracking of supply chains for all livestock products, with particular focus on cattle and beef production in regions where deforestation risks are highest.
