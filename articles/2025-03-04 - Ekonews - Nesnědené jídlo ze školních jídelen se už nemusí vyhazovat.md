Uneaten School Meals Can Now Be Donated to Those in Need

Czech schools can now legally donate leftover food from their cafeterias to charitable organizations, thanks to new guidelines developed by the organization Zachraň jídlo (Save Food) in collaboration with multiple government ministries.

Pilot Project Success

During a three-month pilot program at a Prague elementary school, over 1,500 uneaten meal portions were rescued, including 668 servings of soup, 614 main courses, 155 side dishes, and 85 snacks. These meals went to families living in a nearby shelter, providing meaningful assistance to households in difficult circumstances.

The Problem Being Addressed

Research revealed that approximately one-third of all prepared food in Czech school cafeterias ends up in the trash. The new initiative aims to reduce this substantial food waste while simultaneously supporting vulnerable populations.

Implementation Requirements

Schools must now:
- Obtain approval from their institutional administrator
- Sign a three-party agreement involving the school, its operator, and a charitable organization
- Meet specific hygiene standards and HACCP procedures
- Maintain proper temperature control for both hot and cold meals
- Train staff on proper donation protocols

The organization provides template contracts and guidance documents to facilitate the process. A dedicated app helps track donated meals by type and quantity, supporting transparency and coordination between donors and recipients.
