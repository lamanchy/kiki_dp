# Denmark's Methane Emissions Tax

Denmark has become the first country to impose a tax on methane emissions from livestock. The policy, taking effect in 2030, will charge farmers 300 Danish crowns per ton of CO2 equivalent, rising to 750 crowns by 2035.

## Key Details

**Tax Structure:** Farmers receive a 60% discount initially, with potential additional reductions for implementing mitigation measures like:
- Installing biogas systems for manure
- Using additives to reduce cattle methane emissions

**Government Rationale:** Climate Minister Jeppe Bruus stated the goal is achieving climate targets through significant land transformation, including converting agricultural areas to forests and natural spaces.

**Context:** Denmark has five times more livestock than people, with approximately two-thirds of land dedicated to agriculture. The sector contributes significantly to pollution.

**Industry Response:** Arla Foods, Europe's largest dairy cooperative, supports the compromise as a reasonable environmental solution, though the dairy company views it pragmatically.

**Additional Measures:** Denmark simultaneously announced plans to convert 15% of agricultural land to natural habitats and forests, allocating 6.1 billion dollars to acquire farmland over two decades.
