## Text článku

# Plant-Based Milks - Ecological and Healthy

This Czech article from Svět plodů (World of Fruits) examines sustainable alternatives to dairy milk, addressing both environmental concerns and nutritional considerations.

## Key Environmental Arguments

The article emphasizes that dairy production is resource-intensive: "9 m čtverečních půdy a 628 litrů vody" (9 square meters of land and 628 liters of water) are required per liter of cow's milk. Additionally, livestock accounts for approximately 14.5% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

## Five Recommended Plant-Based Milks

1. **Hazelnut Milk** - Praised as the most sustainable option due to minimal water needs and wind pollination, requiring no bee dependence.

2. **Soy Milk** - The most common alternative, naturally matching cow's milk's protein content at approximately 3g per 100ml, while producing 30% fewer CO2 emissions.

3. **Oat Milk** - Requires 80% less greenhouse gas emissions and 60% less energy compared to dairy milk, plus offers digestive health benefits.

4. **Almond Milk** - Lower carbon footprint and land-use demands, though water consumption and pesticide use remain concerns.

5. **Hemp Milk** - Rich in omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids, grown without pesticides, with entirely biodegradable byproducts.

The article concludes that combining various plant-based options provides optimal nutritional coverage.
