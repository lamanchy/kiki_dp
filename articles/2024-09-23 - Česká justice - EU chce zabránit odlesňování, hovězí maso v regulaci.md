# EU Deforestation Regulation Article

The EU aims to prevent deforestation through a new regulation that the Czech government and businesses say isn't adequately prepared. The government will address the regulation's implementation on Wednesday.

The regulation applies to companies producing, processing, or selling products from coffee, cocoa, palm oil, soy, beef, rubber, and wood. Large and medium-sized firms must comply by December 30 of this year, while small businesses and microenterprises have until June 30, 2025.

Companies must collect and report data on land coordinates where commodities are produced to ensure no connection to deforestation worldwide. The EU will launch its information system approximately one month before compliance deadlines begin—a timeline critics say is insufficient.

Agriculture Minister Marek Výborný (KDU-ČSL) requested a postponement from the European Commission, noting that "firms lack sufficient information" and preparation is inadequate. He's seeking support from incoming Agriculture Commissioner Christopher Hansen.

The ministry estimates 2,120 annual compliance checks costing 97.3 million crowns yearly. Implementation will require hiring 76-96 new employees and transforming the Forest Management Institute into a National Forestry Institute.

Between 1990 and 2020, approximately 420 million hectares of forest were lost globally—equivalent to the EU's size. Czech forests, however, continue expanding.
