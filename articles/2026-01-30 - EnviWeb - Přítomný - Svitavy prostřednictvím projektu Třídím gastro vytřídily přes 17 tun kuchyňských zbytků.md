Svitavy Separates Over 17 Tons of Kitchen Waste Through "Třídím Gastro" Project

Article Summary

The Czech city of Svitavy has successfully separated more than 17 tons of kitchen waste from general municipal waste in just four months through the "Třídím gastro" (I Sort Gastro) initiative run by Energy Financial Group (EFG).

Key Findings

Collection Results:
In the pilot neighborhood of Svitavy Lány, 25 brown collection containers were distributed starting in September. By December alone, the program collected nearly 5.5 tons, averaging 42 kilograms per container. Mayor David Šimek noted: "people are willing to sort waste when cities provide suitable means and clear information" about proper handling.

Processing:
The separated organic waste is transported to biogas facilities where it's converted into biomethane, electricity, and heat—preventing disposal at landfills.

Geographic Expansion:
The project now operates across more than forty municipalities in Moravia, Silesia, and Bohemia.

Critical Success Factor: Public Education

Awareness campaigns prove essential to program success. In areas with educational outreach, kitchen waste collection increased by up to 70 percent. Director Ondřej Černý emphasized: "quality communication with residents significantly increases container productivity."

Environmental Impact

Kitchen waste and spoiled food comprise approximately one-third of standard waste containers, typically ending up unused at landfills without proper sorting.
